Website Evaluations

Dribbble.com (CRAPs Well)

Dribble is an online platform for finding and hiring designers as well as for designers to showcase their work and get hired.

  • Contrast

    The site shows good contrast in using black and pink colors over a white background and grey input elements. Additionally, good spacing is established using a grid layout with sufficient guttering.

  • Repetition

    The site has a consistent color and design theme with black and pink overlaying a grey background. The site also observes a consistent set of fonts and shapes with rounded edges.

  • Alignment

    The site uses a clean grid layout to showcase various featured designers to potential customers as well as incorporates a fixed navigation bar which stays fixed at the top as the user scrolls. Partitioning is also used for organization and emphasis.

  • Proximity

    The site groups featured designs together in a grid, as well as groups related articles from the blog in a column. In this way, navigation is very intuitive.

Johnny Burrito (CRAPs Poorly)

Johnny Burrito is a restaurant specializing in burritos in Central Charlotte.

  • Contrast

    The site uses a lot of bright font and background colors for text throughout the site. The site also features a variety of emphaszied elements meant to catch the attention of the user. However, it comes off as cluttered.

  • Repetition

    The site offers very little repetition and seems to just have content listed with no real methodology as to why. The site does, however, seem to stick to a red-green-blue color scheme over yellow with red buttons which is at least somewhat coherent.

  • Alignment

    The site lists content in a somewhat organized manner, but the organization is not consistent and varies widely by page and content. This is disorienting, but alos makes the page unique.

  • Proximity

    The site, despite seeming to list random stuff, does observe some proximity in its listing of content. However, due to the interspersing of random stuff, it's hard to focus on and process just one section of information.
